Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should
know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic
signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your
bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more
focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Most helmet manufacturers recommend replacing helmets
every 3 to 5 years, even if it hasn’t been involved in a crash.
Over time, the materials (foam, straps, etc.) can degrade due to
exposure to UV light, sweat, and environmental factors,
reducing the helmet's effectiveness.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before riding a bike that has been in storage,
it's not safe to assume it's ready to go; you should
always perform a thorough inspection, checking
key components like tires, brakes, fluids, and the battery,
as they can deteriorate significantly.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Stop at red lights and stop signs, and yield where
required. Do not ride against traffic; it's safer and
usually required by law. If there's a designated bike
lane, use it unless it's unsafe or unavailable.
